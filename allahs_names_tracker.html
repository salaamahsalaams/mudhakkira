Ep 01

How Merciful is the Most Merciful?

Watch

Names Covered

Ar-Raḥmān · Ar-Raḥīm · Ar-Ra'ūf

Theme

Mercy as the governing principle of all creation

The Names

ٱلرَّحْمَٰنُ

Ar-Raḥmān

The Entirely Merciful

ٱلرَّحِيمُ

Ar-Raḥīm

The Especially Merciful

ٱلرَّءُوفُ

Ar-Ra'ūf

The Tenderly Compassionate

Background

Allah opened 113 surahs with Ar-Raḥmān and Ar-Raḥīm — mercy is the first thing He wants you to know about Him. Before creation, He wrote above His Throne: "My mercy prevails over My wrath." He divided mercy into 100 parts, sent just one to earth — enough to sustain all kindness in creation — and kept 99 for believers in the Hereafter.

Distinguishing the Three Names

NameTransliterationScope & Meaning
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنُ Ar-Raḥmān Pours mercy over all of creation in this life — believers and non-believers alike. His mercy governs everything beneath His Throne.
ٱلرَّحِيمُ Ar-Raḥīm Personalises mercy for the believers in the Hereafter. Mentioned 114 times — as if the mercy doubles for those who believe.
ٱلرَّءُوفُ Ar-Ra'ūf The highest form of mercy in Arabic — silent and protective. Allah averting harm before it reaches you, adjusting things in your favour without you even knowing.

Key Points

PointDetail
Mercy frames all other names Even when His power or justice shows, He never stops being Ar-Raḥmān.
Why cruelty exists Not from a shortage of Allah's mercy — from humans failing to be its mirrors. We are trustees of that 1%.
Mercy doesn't cancel justice It would be cruel to the oppressed if the wrongdoer faced no consequence. His mercy is perfect, not lenient in the wrong direction.
How to receive more mercy "The merciful will be shown mercy by Ar-Raḥmān." Show mercy to others and you receive more of His.

Takeaway & Du'ā

Mercy is not Allah's mood — it is His constant nature. Wherever you are, it is never out of your reach.

يَا رَحْمَٰنُ يَا رَحِيمُ يَا رَءُوفُ

O Most Merciful, O Especially Merciful, O Tenderly Compassionate — surround us with the mercy that fills all of creation, grant us its fullness in the Hereafter, and protect us from every harm before it even reaches us.

Ep 02

Who Owns Your Heart?

Watch

Names Covered

Al-Wāḥid · Al-Aḥad · Al-Witr

Theme

Tawḥīd as liberation — unifying the heart under one God

The Names

ٱلْوَاحِدُ

Al-Wāḥid

The One — singular in being

ٱلْأَحَدُ

Al-Aḥad

The Uniquely One — singular in attributes

ٱلْوِتْرُ

Al-Witr

The Odd — the only one of His kind

Background

An Ilāh — a god — is whatever your heart completely revolves around. Allah placed only one heart in your chest because it was made for one master. Whoever owns your heart, owns you.

Distinguishing the Three Names

NameTransliterationMeaning & Distinction
ٱلْوَاحِدُ Al-Wāḥid Singular in being — there is no other God. Negates numbers.
ٱلْأَحَدُ Al-Aḥad Singular in attributes — there is nothing like Him. Bilāl (RA) cried "Aḥad, Aḥad" under torture because this name alone gave him the strength to endure.
ٱلْوِتْرُ Al-Witr The only one of His kind — everything in creation comes in pairs; He alone is unpaired. He loves the odd: Witr prayer, an odd number of dates at iftar, seven circuits of Ṭawāf.

Key Points

PointDetail
Modern idols Career, social media validation, addiction — worship takes new forms even after physical idols are gone.
How shirk is born Born from insecurity or desire — every false god is human fear or longing dressed in divinity.
Allah's response to shirk "I am the least in need of any partner. Whoever does a deed for Me and someone else, I leave him to that someone else." What a loss: to be left to creation after being offered the Creator.
Uniqueness = accessibility His uniqueness means He can be fully present for every person at once — unlimited closeness with unlimited individuality.
Ibn al-Qayyim's formula "For One, be one upon one." Point everything in one direction and your heart stops pulling you in a hundred others.

Takeaway & Du'ā

When others scatter their devotion, direct yours to Him alone — and that is how you become free from all.

يَا وَاحِدُ يَا أَحَدُ يَا وِتْرُ

O The One, O The Uniquely One, O The Singular — unify our hearts in Your devotion, free us from every rival, and let Your oneness be the centre that holds us together.

Ep 03

Who Shows You the Way?

Watch

Names Covered

Al-Hādī · Ar-Rashīd · An-Nūr · Al-Mubīn

Theme

Guidance as a gift — from confusion to clarity to light

The Names

الْهَادِي

Al-Hādī

The Guide

الرَّشِيدُ

Ar-Rashīd

The Director of Right Guidance

النُّورُ

An-Nūr

The Light

الْمُبِينُ

Al-Mubīn

The Self-Evident

Background

You were made to want direction, but guidance isn't automatic — you have to pursue it. He named Himself Al-Hādī, the Guide, not the misguider — guidance is His gift when you turn back, and His justice when you turn away. The door is already open; He just needs you to close every other door along the way.

The Four Names

NameTransliterationScope & Meaning
الْهَادِي Al-Hādī Built guidance into all of creation — atoms orbit, newborns find their mother's chest, birds migrate without a compass. Call out to Him sincerely and He will not turn you away.
الرَّشِيدُ Ar-Rashīd Takes guidance beyond knowledge to instinct — faith starts feeling natural and sin starts feeling wrong, without you having to force it.
النُّورُ An-Nūr The light that takes you from many darknesses into one singular light. As it enters the heart it expands — you can read the Qur'ān a million times and find something new each time.
الْمُبِينُ Al-Mubīn Places signs everywhere so you cannot forget Him. How much you see depends on how much light you carry — the same āyah makes one person shrug and another tremble in awe.

Key Points

PointDetail
Guidance requires effort "Those who strive in Our way, We will certainly guide them." Seeking Al-Hādī sincerely and not being guided is a contradiction.
The first step into a side door Shayṭān doesn't care which door you take — one step in and you can't see the main road. Only Al-Hādī can pull you back out.
Guidance can flip in an instant ʿUmar (RA) left home to kill the Prophet ﷺ and came back as one of the greatest Muslims in history. We see the surface — only Allah sees what is ready.
Knowledge as light Not knowing many narrations — a light placed in the heart in proportion to your steps towards Allah. As you walk, the path lights up beneath you.

Takeaway & Du'ā

There are countless ways to get lost. There is only one way to get home. He already lit the path — keep walking towards Him.

يَا هَادِي يَا رَشِيدُ يَا نُورُ يَا مُبِينُ

O Guide, O Director, O Light, O Self-Evident — guide our steps, make truth feel instinctive, fill our hearts with Your light, and make Your signs clear in everything around us.

Ep 04

Who Never Leaves You?

Watch

Names Covered

Al-Barr · Al-Wālī · Ar-Rafīq

Theme

Allah as the eternal companion — stable, protective, and gentle

The Names

الْبَرُّ

Al-Barr

The Constant Source of All Good

الْوَلِيُّ

Al-Wālī

The Protective Friend

الرَّفِيقُ

Ar-Rafīq

The Gentle Companion

Background

Every companion in our lives is as temporary as the circumstance that brought us together. As Ibn Taymiyyah (RH) said: "Even your shadow leaves you in the dark — but the one who created that shadow remains." Al-Ghazālī (RH) noted that only Allah combines all the qualities of a perfect friend in one: stability, protection, and gentleness.

Distinguishing the Three Names

NameTransliterationScope & Meaning
الْبَرُّ Al-Barr The constant source of all good — as stable as land compared to a tossing sea. You drown depending on others; you find firmness only in Al-Barr.
الْوَلِيُّ Al-Wālī Both guides and guards — even when what you need isn't what you want. Yūsuf (AS) had a loving father, jealous brothers, a prison cell — but Al-Wālī was the only constant throughout. At the end he said: "You are my Wālī in this life and the next."
الرَّفِيقُ Ar-Rafīq Gentle in how He applies firmness — doesn't always remove the mountain, but always softens the path. The Prophet's ﷺ last words were: Allāhumma ar-Rafīq al-Aʿlā — "O Allah, the highest companion."

Key Points

PointDetail
The special Wālī Beyond general guardianship lies a deeper rank: "I become the hearing with which he hears, the sight by which he sees." It comes through private devotion, not public posturing.
The trustworthy handhold Whoever believes in Allah has grasped the handhold that never snaps. He loves when you cling to Him — unlike creation which can tire of your neediness.
Khullah — deepest friendship Only prophets reached the rank of Khalīl. But His closeness to one never takes anything away from His closeness to another.
Ar-Rafīq after death His companionship continues past the grave — sending your deeds to keep you company and angels to reassure you, until He escorts you back to Him.

Takeaway & Du'ā

Every other companion will eventually depart. Until then — and especially then — direct your heart above and say: Allāhumma ar-Rafīq al-Aʿlā.

يَا بَرُّ يَا وَلِيُّ يَا رَفِيقُ

O Constant Good, O Protective Friend, O Gentle Companion — be our stable ground when everything shifts, guard us when we are most vulnerable, and be gentle with us on every step of this journey.

Ep 05

Who Do You Belong To?

Watch

Names Covered

Ar-Rabb · Al-Mawlā · An-Naṣīr · As-Sayyid

Theme

Servitude as liberation — the Lord who raises, guards, and grants victory

The Names

الرَّبُّ

Ar-Rabb

The Lord Who Raises

الْمَوْلَى

Al-Mawlā

The Protecting Master

النَّصِيرُ

An-Naṣīr

The Helper

السَّيِّدُ

As-Sayyid

The Master of All Masters

Background

You don't only want to be His friend — you want to belong to Him. Because friendship brings comfort, but servitude brings purpose. And belonging to Allah is not humiliation; it is the only path to true freedom from everything else.

The Four Names

NameTransliterationScope & Meaning
الرَّبُّ Ar-Rabb Nurtures you toward your full potential — like a farmer tending a plant or a mother raising a child: firm when needed, gentle when needed, always growing you toward what you were made to become.
الْمَوْلَى Al-Mawlā Exclusively on the side of the believers. Az-Zubayr (RA) told his son: "Go to my Mawlā," meaning Allah. Every time a door closed, another opened through du'ā — until every debt was paid.
النَّصِيرُ An-Naṣīr Guarantees victory in the end — "I swear I will deliver for you, even after some time." Sometimes He changes the world around you; sometimes He changes you from within.
السَّيِّدُ As-Sayyid Master of all masters — every ruler leads only within His kingdom. When the Prophet ﷺ was called "our Sayyid," he replied: "The Sayyid is Allah, Blessed and Exalted."

Key Points

PointDetail
Rabb belongs only to Allah The Prophet ﷺ said no one should use the word Rabb for anyone but Allah. It is the most exclusive of His names in its restriction.
Slavery to Allah is freedom "We were sent to free people — from being slaves to other people, to being servants of the Lord of all people." Serving Him sets you free from everything else.
Az-Zubayr's debts and the Mawlā Mountains of debt cleared by turning to Allah as Mawlā — every dead end opened into a door through du'ā, until every debt was fully paid.
A Master who laughs in mercy He laughs not to mock but in mercy — like a mother smiling at a child who lost sight of her, already knowing she was right there all along.

Takeaway & Du'ā

Belonging to Him is not a burden — it is the only path to true freedom from all else.

يَا رَبُّ يَا مَوْلَى يَا نَصِيرُ يَا سَيِّدُ

O Lord, O Protecting Master, O Helper, O Master of all — raise us as only You can, protect us as only You do, help us when we cannot help ourselves, and let our honour be only in belonging to You.
