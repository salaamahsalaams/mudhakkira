Ep 01

How Merciful is the Most Merciful?

Names Covered

Ar-Raḥmān · Ar-Raḥīm · Ar-Ra'ūf

Source

Qur'an · Ḥadīth Qudsī · Ibn al-Qayyim

Theme

Mercy as the governing principle of all creation

The Names

ٱلرَّحْمَٰنُ

Ar-Raḥmān

The Entirely Merciful

ٱلرَّحِيمُ

Ar-Raḥīm

The Especially Merciful

ٱلرَّءُوفُ

Ar-Ra'ūf

The Tenderly Compassionate

Background

Out of all His names, Allah chose Ar-Raḥmān and Ar-Raḥīm as the opening of 113 surahs — because mercy is the first door to knowing Him. Before creation existed, He wrote above His throne: Inna raḥmatī ghalabat ghaḍabī — "Verily, My mercy prevails over My wrath." Ibn al-Qayyim said this means forgiveness is more beloved to Him than vengeance, mercy more beloved than punishment, giving more beloved than withholding.

When the soul of Adam (AS) first entered his body and he sneezed, the very first words he heard from his Lord were: Yarḥamuka Allāh — "May Allah have mercy on you." Mercy was humanity's welcome into existence.

The Prophet ﷺ said Allah created 100 portions of mercy and sent just one down to Earth. Through that single 1%, the entire world is sustained — every act of kindness between humans, animals, and creation. The remaining 99% is reserved for the believers in the Hereafter.

Distinguishing the Three Names

NameTransliterationScope & Meaning
ٱلرَّحْمَٰنُ Ar-Raḥmān The entirely merciful — pours mercy over all of creation in this life, deserving and undeserving alike. Mentioned in the Qur'an 57 times. The name paired with the Throne: Ar-Raḥmānu 'ala al-'arshi istawā — His mercy governs everything beneath it.
ٱلرَّحِيمُ Ar-Raḥīm The especially merciful — personalises mercy for the believers in the Hereafter. Mentioned 114 times (exactly double Ar-Raḥmān), as if to say: the mercy doubles for those who believe. Wa kāna bil-mu'minīna raḥīmā.
ٱلرَّءُوفُ Ar-Ra'ūf The tenderly compassionate — the highest form of mercy in Arabic. Operates silently: Allah protecting you before you know you needed it, averting harm before it lands, adjusting circumstances in your favour. Almost always paired with Ar-Raḥīm in the Qur'an.

Key Points

PointDetail
Mercy frames all other names Ar-Raḥmān can be added to every other name. He is merciful with His knowledge, His sight, His power. Even when Al-Jabbār and Al-Qahhār show, He never stops being Ar-Raḥmān.
Why cruelty exists Hunger exists not from a shortage of food but from a failure to share. Cruelty exists not from a shortage of Allah's mercy, but from a failure of humans to be its mirrors. We are the trustees of that 1%.
Mercy doesn't cancel justice It would be cruel to the oppressed if the oppressor and victim were treated identically. His mercy is not limited — it is perfect. The question isn't "how can a merciful God send people to Hell?" — it's "why would people choose Hell over a merciful God?"
How to receive more mercy Ar-rāḥimūna yarḥamuhum ar-Raḥmān — the merciful will be shown mercy by Ar-Raḥmān. The nearest to Allah are those who combine Ra'ūf and Raḥīm, as the Prophet ﷺ himself was: bil-mu'minīna ra'ūfun raḥīm.

Takeaway & Du'ā

Mercy is not Allah's mood — it is His constant nature. Wherever you are, it is not out of your reach, because you are something and His mercy encompasses everything.

يَا رَحْمَٰنُ — يَا رَحِيمُ — يَا رَءُوفُ

Yā Raḥmān — embrace every part of me: my past with its scars, my present with its needs, my future with its uncertainty.

Yā Raḥīm — let me taste the mercy You keep for those who return. Raise me to the highest place in Paradise despite my lows.

Yā Ra'ūf — cover me from storms I don't see coming. Let Your mercy reach me in ways I'll only understand when I finally meet You.

Ep 02

Who Owns Your Heart?

Names Covered

Al-Wāḥid · Al-Aḥad · Al-Witr

Source

Qur'an · Ḥadīth Qudsī · Ibn al-Qayyim · Ibn Taymiyyah

Theme

Tawḥīd as liberation — unifying the heart under one God

The Names

ٱلْوَاحِدُ

Al-Wāḥid

The One — singular in being

ٱلْأَحَدُ

Al-Aḥad

The Uniquely One — singular in attributes

ٱلْوِتْرُ

Al-Witr

The Odd — the only one of His kind

Background

The root of Ilāh — as Ibn al-Qayyim explains — is that which hearts adore with complete love, reverence, awe, hope and fear. The heart was designed to seek one master. Allah says: Mā jaʿala Allāhu li-rajulin min qalbayni fī jawfih — Allah has not placed two hearts in a single man's chest. Whoever owns your heart, owns you.

When the Prophet ﷺ met the father of ʿImrān ibn Ḥusayn, he asked how many gods he worshipped. The man said seven — six on earth, one in heaven. The Prophet ﷺ asked: "But which one do you turn to in hope and fear when everything falls apart?" He said: "The one in the heavens." The Prophet ﷺ said: "Then leave the other six and worship Him alone." He didn't argue theology — he spoke directly to the unsatisfying experience of shirk.

Distinguishing the Three Names

NameTransliterationMeaning & Distinction
ٱلْوَاحِدُ Al-Wāḥid Singular in His being — negates numbers. There is no God but Him. (You can say rajulun wāḥid, one man, or alf wāḥid, a thousand ones — Wāḥid admits counting.)
ٱلْأَحَدُ Al-Aḥad Singular in His attributes — negates likeness. There is no God like Him. You cannot say rajulun aḥad — Aḥad means no comparison exists. Sūrah al-Ikhlāṣ says Allāhu Aḥad, not Allāhu Wāḥid — not just "one God" but "uniquely one God." Bilāl (RA) repeated Aḥad, Aḥad under torture — knowing this name alone gave him strength enough to die for it.
ٱلْوِتْرُ Al-Witr The only one of His kind — negates pair. Everything in creation comes in pairs: night/day, land/sea, male/female. Allah alone is Al-Witr, truly unpaired. He loves the odd: Witr prayer, odd number of dates at iftar, three sips of water, seven circuits of Tawāf — every act a reminder that He alone stands unmatched.

Key Points

PointDetail
Modern idols The Prophet ﷺ said: "Miserable is the slave of the dirham, the dinar, the dollar, the pound." Career worship, addiction to sin, deriving self-worth from social media validation — worship doesn't end when physical idols are broken. It takes on new forms. Today idols wear designer brands and blue checkmarks.
How shirk is born Shirk is never rational — it's born from insecurity, desire, or imitation. Historically: those who worshipped nature couldn't imagine one power sustaining it all; those who worshipped good & evil couldn't grasp one God governing both; those who shaped God in their image couldn't relate to a God they couldn't see. Every false god is human insecurity, desire, or corruption dressed in divinity.
Allah's response to shirk In a Ḥadīth Qudsī: Anā aghnā al-shurakā'i ʿan al-shirk — "I am the least in need of any partner. Whoever does a deed seeking Me and someone else, I leave him to that someone else." What a punishment: to be left to creation after being offered the Creator.
Uniqueness = accessibility Allah's uniqueness (Al-Witr) doesn't make Him distant — it makes Him unlike creation. A friend drowning in the same crisis can only help so much. Allah, uniquely unlike creation, can be close to every creature at once. Unlimited uniqueness with unlimited accessibility.
Ibn al-Qayyim's formula Fa-li-wāḥidin kun wāḥidan fī wāḥid — "For One, be one upon one" — the single path of truth. Unify your pursuits, your heart, your devotion — all directed to Al-Wāḥid Al-Aḥad.

Takeaway & Du'ā

With Al-Wāḥid you negate all others. With Al-Aḥad you affirm His transcendence. With Al-Witr you recognise His complete singularity. When others scatter their devotion, you direct yours to Him alone — and that is how you become free from all.

يَا إِلَٰهِي — يَا وَاحِدُ — يَا أَحَدُ — يَا وِتْرُ

Yā Ilāhī — pull me away from every false worship, from anything that divides my devotion, until nothing fills my longing but You.

Yā Wāḥid — unify my pursuits into a single direction. When the world pulls me apart, centre me in Your oneness.

Yā Aḥad — open my heart to see that nothing compares, so I am never overly impressed by anyone but You.

Yā Witr — when I stand alone at the close of the night, let my solitude remind me of Your singular majesty. Make my heart distinct by its devotion.
