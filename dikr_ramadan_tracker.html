Ep 01

This Destroys Every Problem

Watch

Dhikr

Lā Ḥawla wa Lā Quwwata illā Billāh

Theme

Surrender, transformation & reliance on Allah alone

The Dhikr

لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

Lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh

There is no transformation or power except through Allah

Note: Ḥawl derives from taḥwīl — transformation, the shift from one state (ḥāl) to another. The phrase means: no change of state, and no strength to do anything, is possible except through Allah.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ called it "a treasure from the treasures of Paradise" and taught it to 19 companions by name. It is also one of the five al-Baqiyāt al-Ṣāliḥāt — the remaining righteous deeds — mentioned in Sūrah al-Kahf (18:46), alongside SubḥānAllāh, Alḥamdulillāh, Lā ilāha illAllāh, and Allāhu Akbar.

When & Why to Say It

PointDetail
Tawḥīd of Help Just as Lā ilāha illAllāh declares Allah the only object of worship, this phrase declares Him the only source of help — mirroring Iyyāka nastaʿīn in al-Fātiḥah.
Allah's response When you say it, Allah declares: "My servant has submitted and surrendered." (Al-Bayhaqi)
At the Adhān When you hear ḥayya ʿala al-ṣalāh, respond with this — admitting you cannot move from heedlessness to prayer without His help.
Leaving the house Say it with Bismillāh and tawakkul when leaving home — the Prophet ﷺ promised: you will be guided, sufficed, protected, and Shayṭān will turn away.
For hardship & sin Ibn Taymiyyah (RH): "Let his constant refuge be this phrase — through it, terrors are met, burdens are carried, and lofty stations are reached."
Against the evil eye Say Māshā'Allāh, lā quwwata illā billāh (Qur'an 18:39) when you see a blessing — to protect it and yourself.

Takeaway

Every transformation — from sickness to health, from sin to obedience — comes only from Allah. Say it when overwhelmed, exhausted, tempted, or struck by news. It is an act of surrender that invites His help.

Ep 02

The Du'ā of Yūnus (AS)

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā of Yūnus — 3 phrases

Theme

Distress, Tawḥīd, glorification & confession as the path to relief

The Dhikr

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ Lā ilāha illā anta There is nothing worthy of worship except You
سُبْحَانَكَ Subḥānaka Exalted are You — free from all imperfection
إِنِّي كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ Innī kuntu mina al-ẓālimīn Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers

Background

Yūnus (AS) was sent to a city of over 100,000 people — possibly the largest on earth at the time. After years of rejection, he left without Allah's permission. Meanwhile, Allah cast repentance into the people's hearts and they all wept and turned to Him — the only city in history whose faith, after seeing the signs of punishment, was accepted.

Yūnus (AS), now on a storm-struck ship, had lots drawn three times — each time falling on him. Thrown overboard and swallowed by a whale, in total darkness he realised he was alive, prostrated to Allah, and made this du'ā.

The Four Elements

ElementExplanation
Tawḥīd Only Allah has power and only He deserves to be called upon. Nothing else can save you.
Tasbīḥ Even in darkest distress, affirming: "What happened to me is not injustice from You. You are perfect." You exalt Allah above the situation, not despite it.
Servitude Acknowledging complete dependence on Allah — you are His servant in need, not an equal making demands.
Confession Admitting wrongdoing without explicitly asking forgiveness — the request is eloquently implied in the description of your own state.
The promise The Prophet ﷺ: "No Muslim supplicates with it for anything ever except that Allah responds." (Al-Tirmidhī)
Your whale Every believer has a whale — fear, anxiety, addiction, negligence. Allah's rescue of Yūnus is His template for saving all believers in every time and place (21:88).

Takeaway

Say these three phrases when you feel trapped, hopeless, or stuck. You are declaring Tawḥīd, exalting Allah, acknowledging your need, and confessing your shortcomings — the most direct path to relief from any hardship.

Ep 03

The Du'ā to Say at Iftar

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā at Breaking the Fast — 3 phrases

Theme

Gratitude, humility & the principle that hardship passes but reward remains

The Dhikr

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
ذَهَبَ الظَّمَأُ Dhahaba al-ẓama'u The thirst is gone
وَابْتَلَّتِ الْعُرُوقُ Wabtallatil-'urūqu The veins are moistened
وَثَبَتَ الْأَجْرُ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ Wa thabata al-ajru inshā'Allāh And the reward is confirmed, if Allah wills

Source: Narrated by Ibn ʿUmar (RA), reported by Abu Dawud.

Background

Before you eat at iftar, say this. The Prophet ﷺ taught us to mark the end of the fast not just with food but with acknowledgement — the thirst passed, the body is being restored, and the reward was hopefully earned. The inshā'Allāh at the end is a deliberate reminder of humility: you completed the action, but only Allah knows if He accepted it.

Breaking It Down

PointDetail
Even if not thirsty Say the du'ā regardless — you are acknowledging the principle of fasting (that it involves thirst), not just your personal experience that particular day.
Why "inshā'Allāh" for the reward You don't know if your fast was spiritually intact. The Prophet ﷺ said: "How many a person fasts and gets nothing except hunger and thirst" — because they violated the fast with backbiting, lying, or insincerity.
The law of worship vs sin Hardship of obedience is temporary — its reward is permanent. Pleasure of sin is temporary — its consequences are lasting. This du'ā puts that reality into words at the moment you feel it most.
Make du'ā all day The fasting person has an accepted du'ā throughout the fast, not just at iftar. Don't wait for the last moments — call on Allah while you're driving, sitting, or waiting.
Be present at iftar Iftar is a social event, but before the food arrives — take that moment. Say the du'ā mindfully. Ask for acceptance. Then eat.

Takeaway

This du'ā is the spiritual close to your day of fasting. Say it before you reach for food — not as a habit, but as a genuine moment of acknowledgement before Allah.

Ep 04

This Du'ā Covers Everything You Could Need

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā al-Qunūt — the Witr supplication

Theme

Six comprehensive requests: guidance, wellness, Allah's friendship, baraka, protection & praise

The Dhikr

اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ فَإِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلَا يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ وَلَا يَعِزُّ مَنْ عَادَيْتَ تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ

Allāhumma ihdinī fīman hadayt, wa ʿāfinī fīman ʿāfayt, wa tawallanī fīman tawallayt, wa bārik lī fīmā aʿṭayt, wa qinī sharra mā qaḍayt. Fa-innaka taqḍī wa lā yuqḍā ʿalayk. Wa innahū lā yadhillu man wālayt, wa lā yaʿizzu man ʿādayt. Tabārakta rabbanā wa taʿālayt.

O Allah, guide me amongst those You've guided; grant me wellness amongst those You've given wellness; befriend me amongst those You've befriended; bless me in what You've given me; protect me from the evil of what You've decreed. For You decree and none decrees over You. None is humiliated whom You support, and none is honoured whom You oppose. Blessed are You, our Lord, and Exalted.

Source: Narrated by al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī (RA), reported by Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhī.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ personally taught this du'ā to his grandson al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī (RA) — who was under 10 years old — for his Witr prayer. It shows that the Prophet ﷺ invested in his family's children, not just adults. Six requests in one du'ā. Less than 30 seconds to say.

Breaking Down the Du'ā

RequestWhat It Means
اهْدِنِي — Guide me Guidance has multiple levels — to good deeds, to acting on what you know, to better actions, to consistency, and to die upon goodness. You need all of them at every stage of life.
عَافِنِي — Give me wellness Comprehensive well-being — from disease, from bad character, from innovation, from misguidance. Not just the body; the heart and religion too.
تَوَلَّنِي — Befriend me Asking Allah to take charge of your affairs and not leave you to yourself — even for a moment's blink. The believers are the allies of Allah.
بَارِكْ لِي — Bless me Baraka is quality over quantity. Some live 100 years and accomplish nothing; others leave a legacy at 20. Make my limited time and resources go far.
قِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ — Protect me from evil Evil is in creation, not in Allah's action. When rain causes a flood, the evil is in the circumstance — not in Allah decreeing it. You're asking Him to turn that evil away from you.
لَا يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ — None humiliated whom You support Physical harm is not humiliation. Companions were wounded and killed, yet Allah said: "You are superior if you are believers." Humiliation is internal — and it only comes from disobedience.

Takeaway

This is the only sunnah-legislated du'ā for the Witr prayer — short, comprehensive, and profound. Say it every Witr, not just in Ramadan.

Ep 05

The Master of Seeking Forgiveness

Watch

Dhikr

Sayyid al-Istighfār — Master of Seeking Forgiveness

Theme

Complete framework of repentance — Lordship, servitude, covenant, confession & forgiveness

The Dhikr

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ

Allāhumma anta rabbī, lā ilāha illā anta, khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka, wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿt. Aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿt. Abū'u laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya wa abū'u bidhanbī. Faghfirlī fa-innahū lā yaghfiru al-dhunūba illā ant.

O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no deity worthy of worship except You. You created me and I am Your servant, and I am faithful to my covenant and promise to You as much as I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me, and I acknowledge my sin. So forgive me — for indeed none forgives sins except You.

Source: Narrated by Shaddād ibn Aws (RA), Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (7393).

Background

The Prophet ﷺ called this Sayyid al-Istighfār — the master of all forms of seeking forgiveness. It is not just "sorry" or "forgive me." It is a complete picture of your relationship with Allah: who He is, who you are, what He has given you, and what you have done in return. Whoever says it during the day with firm faith and dies before evening, or says it at night and dies before morning, is promised paradise.

Breaking Down the Du'ā

PhraseWhat It Means
أَنْتَ رَبِّي — You are my Lord Starting with acknowledgement — He sustains you, manages your life, provides and protects. He is running things, not you.
لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ — No deity except You Moving from Lordship to worship — since You are my creator and provider, You alone deserve my worship. Not career, not status, not desires.
أَنَا عَبْدُكَ — I am Your servant Declaring your position honestly — not equal, not independent. Every person, regardless of title or achievement, says in this du'ā: "I am nothing but Your servant."
عَلَى عَهْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ — As much as I can Humility — not "I'm doing well," but "I'm trying with my weak capacity." Without this phrase, you'd be lying. None of us can fulfil Allah's rights the way they deserve.
أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ — I seek refuge from my deeds Running TO Allah because of your sins — not away from Him. You're not fleeing from Allah; you're fleeing to Him. Where else would you go?
أَبُوءُ بِنِعْمَتِكَ...وَبِذَنْبِي — I acknowledge favour and sin The contrast that changes everything — from You is every blessing, from me is every shortcoming. When you truly internalise this, arrogance becomes impossible.

Takeaway

Say it every morning and every evening — not as a habit, but as a moment of standing before Allah with your full reality. It is not just seeking forgiveness; it is a complete renewal of your relationship with Him.
