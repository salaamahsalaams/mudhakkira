Ep 01

This Destroys EVERY Problem

Dhikr

La Hawla wa La Quwwata illa Billah

Source

Bukhari & Muslim · Imam Ahmad · Al-Bayhaqi

Theme

Surrender, transformation & reliance on Allah alone

The Dhikr

لاَ حَوْلَ وَلاَ قُوَّةَ إِلاَّ بِاللَّهِ

Lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh

There is no transformation or power except through Allah

Note: Ḥawl derives from taḥwīl — transformation, the shift from one ḥāl (state) to another. The phrase means: no change of state, and no strength to do anything, is possible except through Allah.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ called it "a treasure from the treasures of Paradise" and specifically taught it to 19 companions. It is also listed by the Prophet ﷺ as one of the al-Baqiyāt al-Ṣāliḥāt — the "remaining righteous deeds" mentioned in Sūrah al-Kahf (18:46), alongside SubḥānAllah, Alḥamdulillah, Lā ilāha illAllāh, and Allāhu Akbar.

When & Why to Use It

PointDetail
Tawḥīd of Help Just as Lā ilāha illAllāh declares Allah the only object of worship, this phrase declares Him the only source of help — mirroring Iyyāka nastaʿīn in al-Fātiḥah.
Allah's Response When a person says it, Allah declares: "My slave has submitted and surrendered." (Al-Bayhaqi — the Prophet ﷺ said: aslama ʿabdī wa-staslam)
Response to Adhān When you hear ḥayya ʿala al-ṣalāh / al-falāḥ, respond with this phrase — admitting you cannot move from heedlessness to prayer without Allah's help.
Leaving the House Bismillāh, tawakkaltu ʿala Allāh, wa lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh — the Prophet ﷺ said: you will be guided, sufficed, protected, and Shayṭān will turn away.
For Hardship Ibn Taymiyyah: "Let his constant refuge be this phrase — through it, terrors are met, burdens are carried, and lofty stations are reached."
For the Evil Eye Qur'an 18:39 — say Māshā'Allāh, lā quwwata illā billāh when entering a blessing, to protect it and yourself.

Takeaway

Every transformation — from sickness to health, weakness to strength, sin to obedience — comes only from Allah. Say it when overwhelmed, exhausted, tempted, or struck by news. It is an act of surrender that invites divine assistance.

Ep 02

The Power of the Du'ā of Yūnus

Dhikr

Du'ā of Yūnus (Dhu al-Nūn) — 3 phrases

Source

Qur'an 21:87–88 · Al-Tirmidhī

Theme

Distress, Tawḥīd, glorification & confession as path to relief

The Dhikr

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ Lā ilāha illā anta There is nothing worthy of worship except You
سُبْحَانَكَ Subḥānaka Exalted are You — free from all imperfection
إِنِّي كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ Innī kuntu mina al-ẓālimīn Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers

Background

Yūnus (AS) was sent to Nineveh — a city of 100,000+, possibly the largest on earth at the time. After years of rejection he left without Allah's permission. Allah then softened the people's hearts and they repented en masse: the only city in history whose faith after seeing a sign of punishment was accepted (10:98).

Meanwhile Yūnus was on a storm-struck ship. Lots were drawn three times — each time falling on him. Thrown overboard, he was swallowed by a whale. Waking in complete darkness, he realised he was alive, prostrated to Allah, and made this du'ā.

The Four Elements of the Du'ā

ElementExplanation
1 — Tawḥīd Declaring that only Allah has power and only He deserves to be called upon.
2 — Tasbīḥ Glorifying Allah above every imperfection — even in darkest distress affirming: "What happened to me is not injustice from You. You are perfect."
3 — Servitude Acknowledging complete dependence and need for Allah.
4 — Confession Admitting wrongdoing without explicitly asking forgiveness — the request is eloquently implied in the description of one's own state.
The Promise The Prophet ﷺ: "No Muslim supplicates with it for anything ever except that Allah responds." (Al-Tirmidhī)
Universal Application Every believer has a "whale" — fear, anxiety, addiction, negligence. Allah's rescue of Yūnus is His template for saving all believers in every time and place (21:88).

Takeaway

Say these three phrases when you feel trapped, hopeless, or stuck. You are declaring Tawḥīd, glorifying Allah, acknowledging your need, and confessing your shortcomings — the most direct path to relief from any hardship.
