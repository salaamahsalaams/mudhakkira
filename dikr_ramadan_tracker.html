Ep 01

This Destroys Every Problem

Watch

Dhikr

Lā Ḥawla wa Lā Quwwata illā Billāh

Theme

Surrender, transformation & reliance on Allah alone

The Dhikr

لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

Lā ḥawla wa lā quwwata illā billāh

There is no transformation or power except through Allah

Note: Ḥawl derives from taḥwīl — transformation, the shift from one state (ḥāl) to another. The phrase means: no change of state, and no strength to do anything, is possible except through Allah.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ called it "a treasure from the treasures of Paradise" and taught it to 19 companions by name. It is one of the five remaining righteous deeds alongside SubhanAllah, Alhamdulillah, La ilaha illAllah, and Allahu Akbar.

When & Why to Say It

PointDetail
It declares Allah as your only source of help Just as La ilaha illAllah makes Him the only object of worship, this phrase makes Him the only source of help. When you say it, Allah responds: "My servant has submitted and surrendered."
Say it at the adhan, when leaving home, and when overwhelmed Respond to "come to prayer" with it — admitting you cannot move from heedlessness to worship without Him. Say it with Bismillah when leaving home for guidance and protection. Say it when overwhelmed, exhausted, or struck by hardship.
It conquers sin and protects from the evil eye Ibn Taymiyyah said: "Let his constant refuge be this phrase — through it, terrors are met, burdens are carried, and lofty stations are reached." Say Masha'Allah la quwwata illa billah when you see a blessing — to protect it.

Takeaway

Every transformation — from sickness to health, from sin to obedience — comes only from Allah. Say it when overwhelmed, exhausted, tempted, or struck by news. It is an act of surrender that invites His help.

Ep 02

The Du'ā of Yūnus (AS)

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā of Yūnus — 3 phrases

Theme

Distress, Tawḥīd, glorification & confession as the path to relief

The Dhikr

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ Lā ilāha illā anta There is nothing worthy of worship except You
سُبْحَانَكَ Subḥānaka Exalted are You — free from all imperfection
إِنِّي كُنْتُ مِنَ الظَّالِمِينَ Innī kuntu mina al-ẓālimīn Indeed, I have been of the wrongdoers

Background

Yunus (AS) left his people after years of rejection — without Allah's permission. He boarded a ship, lots were drawn three times and each time fell on him. Thrown overboard and swallowed by a whale, in total darkness he prostrated and made this du'a. Allah saved him.

What Makes This Du'a Work

ElementExplanation
It combines four things at once Tawhid (only Allah can save you), glorification (You are perfect — what happened is not injustice from You), servitude (I am dependent on You), and confession (I wronged myself). This combination is the most direct path to relief.
The promise is absolute The Prophet ﷺ said: "No Muslim supplicates with it for anything ever except that Allah responds." Not sometimes — always. (Al-Tirmidhi)
Everyone has a whale Fear, anxiety, addiction, negligence — whatever has swallowed you. Allah said He saved Yunus "and that is how We save the believers" — in every time and place (21:88).

Takeaway

Say these three phrases when you feel trapped, hopeless, or stuck. You are declaring Tawḥīd, exalting Allah, acknowledging your need, and confessing your shortcomings — the most direct path to relief from any hardship.

Ep 03

The Du'ā to Say at Iftar

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā at Breaking the Fast — 3 phrases

Theme

Gratitude, humility & the principle that hardship passes but reward remains

The Dhikr

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
ذَهَبَ الظَّمَأُ Dhahaba al-ẓama'u The thirst is gone
وَابْتَلَّتِ الْعُرُوقُ Wabtallatil-'urūqu The veins are moistened
وَثَبَتَ الْأَجْرُ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ Wa thabata al-ajru inshā'Allāh And the reward is confirmed, if Allah wills

Source: Narrated by Ibn ʿUmar (RA), reported by Abu Dawud.

Background

Say this before you eat at iftar. The thirst passed, the body is being restored, and you hope the reward was earned. The "insha'Allah" at the end is humility — you completed the action, but only Allah knows if He accepted it.

Breaking It Down

PointDetail
Why "insha'Allah" for the reward You don't know if your fast was spiritually intact. The Prophet ﷺ said: "How many a person fasts and gets nothing from their fast except hunger and thirst" — because they violated it with backbiting, lying, or insincerity.
Hardship of worship is temporary, its reward is permanent The thirst passes within minutes of iftar. But the reward remains. Sins work the opposite way — the pleasure is temporary, the consequences are lasting. This du'a puts that reality into words.
Make du'a throughout the entire fast The fasting person has an accepted du'a throughout the fast, not just at iftar. Don't wait for the last moments — call on Allah while driving, sitting, or waiting. Be present at iftar and say this du'a before reaching for food.

Takeaway

This du'ā is the spiritual close to your day of fasting. Say it before you reach for food — not as a habit, but as a genuine moment of acknowledgement before Allah.

Ep 04

This Du'ā Covers Everything You Could Need

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā al-Qunūt — the Witr supplication

Theme

Six comprehensive requests: guidance, wellness, Allah's friendship, baraka, protection & praise

The Dhikr

اللَّهُمَّ اهْدِنِي فِيمَنْ هَدَيْتَ وَعَافِنِي فِيمَنْ عَافَيْتَ وَتَوَلَّنِي فِيمَنْ تَوَلَّيْتَ وَبَارِكْ لِي فِيمَا أَعْطَيْتَ وَقِنِي شَرَّ مَا قَضَيْتَ فَإِنَّكَ تَقْضِي وَلَا يُقْضَى عَلَيْكَ وَإِنَّهُ لَا يَذِلُّ مَنْ وَالَيْتَ وَلَا يَعِزُّ مَنْ عَادَيْتَ تَبَارَكْتَ رَبَّنَا وَتَعَالَيْتَ

Allāhumma ihdinī fīman hadayt, wa ʿāfinī fīman ʿāfayt, wa tawallanī fīman tawallayt, wa bārik lī fīmā aʿṭayt, wa qinī sharra mā qaḍayt. Fa-innaka taqḍī wa lā yuqḍā ʿalayk. Wa innahū lā yadhillu man wālayt, wa lā yaʿizzu man ʿādayt. Tabārakta rabbanā wa taʿālayt.

O Allah, guide me amongst those You've guided; give me wellness amongst those You've given wellness to; protect me among those You've protected; bless me in what has been given to me by You; and protect me from any evil You've decreed. For You decree in truth and none decree over You. None can honour who You oppose, and none can humiliate who You support. Blessed are You, our Lord, and Exalted.

Source: Narrated by al-Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī (RA), reported by Abu Dawud and al-Tirmidhī.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ personally taught this du'a to his grandson al-Hasan ibn Ali (RA) — who was under 10 years old — for his Witr prayer. This is the only sunnah-legislated du'a for Witr. Six requests, less than 30 seconds to say.

Breaking Down the Du'ā

PhraseWhat It Means
Guide me Guidance has multiple levels — to good deeds, to acting on what you know, to better actions, to consistency, and to die upon goodness. You need all of them at every stage of life.
Give me wellness Comprehensive well-being — from disease, from bad character, from innovation, from misguidance. Not just the body; the heart and religion too.
Befriend me Asking Allah to take charge of your affairs and not leave you to yourself — even for a moment. The believers are the allies of Allah.
Bless me in what You have given Baraka is quality over quantity. Some live 100 years and accomplish nothing; others leave a legacy at 20. Make my limited time and resources go far.
Protect me from the evil You've decreed Evil is in creation, not in Allah's action. When rain causes a flood, the evil is in the circumstance — not in Allah decreeing it. You're asking Him to turn that evil away from you.
For You decree and none decrees over You This is the reasoning behind the request. No one can reverse Your judgment. Nothing You said will happen can be made not to happen. Nothing in creation can obligate You.
None is humiliated whom You support Physical harm is not humiliation — humiliation is internal, a degradation of the soul that comes only from disobedience. Whoever Allah supports is honoured regardless of what happens to the body.
None is honoured whom You oppose Whoever Allah takes as an enemy is lowly — even if the world celebrates them with wealth, power, and fame. True honour belongs only to the people of faith.
Blessed are You, our Lord, and Exalted You close with praise, not another request. You are the source of all goodness and You are exalted above all. The du'a begins with your need and closes with His greatness.

Takeaway

This is the only sunnah-legislated du'ā for the Witr prayer — short, comprehensive, and profound. Say it every Witr, not just in Ramadan.

Ep 05

The Master of Seeking Forgiveness

Watch

Dhikr

Sayyid al-Istighfār — Master of Seeking Forgiveness

Theme

Complete framework of repentance — Lordship, servitude, covenant, confession & forgiveness

The Dhikr

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ وَأَبُوءُ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ

Allāhumma anta rabbī, lā ilāha illā anta, khalaqtanī wa anā ʿabduka, wa anā ʿalā ʿahdika wa waʿdika mastaṭaʿt. Aʿūdhu bika min sharri mā ṣanaʿt. Abū'u laka biniʿmatika ʿalayya wa abū'u bidhanbī. Faghfirlī fa-innahū lā yaghfiru al-dhunūba illā ant.

O Allah, You are my Lord. There is no deity worthy of worship except You. You created me and I am Your servant, and I am faithful to my covenant and promise to You as much as I can. I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done. I acknowledge Your favour upon me, and I acknowledge my sin. So forgive me — for indeed none forgives sins except You.

Source: Narrated by Shaddād ibn Aws (RA), Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī (7393).

Background

The Prophet ﷺ called this Sayyid al-Istighfār — the master of all forms of seeking forgiveness. It is not just "sorry" or "forgive me." It is a complete picture of your relationship with Allah: who He is, who you are, what He has given you, and what you have done in return. Whoever says it during the day with firm faith and dies before evening, or says it at night and dies before morning, is promised paradise.

Breaking Down the Du'ā

PhraseWhat It Means
You are my Lord Starting with acknowledgement — He sustains you, manages your life, provides and protects. He is running things, not you.
No deity except You Moving from Lordship to worship — since You are my creator and provider, You alone deserve my worship. Not career, not status, not desires.
I am Your servant Declaring your position honestly — not equal, not independent. Every person, regardless of title or achievement, says: "I am nothing but Your servant."
I am faithful to my covenant as much as I can Humility — not "I'm doing well," but "I'm trying with my weak capacity." Without this phrase, you'd be lying. None of us can fulfil Allah's rights the way they deserve.
I seek refuge in You from the evil of what I have done Running TO Allah because of your sins — not away from Him. You're not fleeing from Allah; you're fleeing to Him. Where else would you go?
I acknowledge Your favour and I acknowledge my sin From You is goodness and every blessing; from me is sin and wrongdoing. When you truly internalise this, arrogance becomes impossible. You are the one who keeps falling short and Allah is the one who keeps giving anyway.
So forgive me, for none forgives sins except You The request comes only at the end — after acknowledging His lordship, His right to be worshipped, His blessings, and your own sins. That sequence is deliberate. You close with certainty: no saint, no scholar, no intermediary — only He forgives.

Takeaway

Say it every morning and every evening — not as a habit, but as a moment of standing before Allah with your full reality. It is not just seeking forgiveness; it is a complete renewal of your relationship with Him.

Ep 06

This Du'ā Feeds Your Soul When It's Starving

Watch

Dhikr

Du'ā for Anxiety & Grief

Theme

Removing worry and sadness by connecting to the Quran

The Dhikr

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي عَبْدُكَ ابْنُ عَبْدِكَ ابْنُ أَمَتِكَ نَاصِيَتِي بِيَدِكَ مَاضٍ فِيَّ حُكْمُكَ عَدْلٌ فِيَّ قَضَاؤُكَ أَسْأَلُكَ بِكُلِّ اسْمٍ هُوَ لَكَ سَمَّيْتَ بِهِ نَفْسَكَ أَوْ عَلَّمْتَهُ أَحَدًا مِنْ خَلْقِكَ أَوْ أَنْزَلْتَهُ فِي كِتَابِكَ أَوِ اسْتَأْثَرْتَ بِهِ فِي عِلْمِ الْغَيْبِ عِنْدَكَ أَنْ تَجْعَلَ الْقُرْآنَ رَبِيعَ قَلْبِي وَنُورَ صَدْرِي وَجَلَاءَ حُزْنِي وَذَهَابَ هَمِّي

Allāhumma innī ʿabduka ibnu ʿabdika ibnu amatika, nāṣiyatī biyadika, māḍin fiyya ḥukmuka, ʿadlun fiyya qaḍā'uka. As'aluka bikulli ismin huwa laka, sammayta bihi nafsaka, aw ʿallamtahu aḥadan min khalqika, aw anzaltahu fī kitābika, aw ista'tharta bihi fī ʿilm il-ghaybi ʿindaka, an tajʿal al-Qur'āna rabīʿa qalbī, wa nūra ṣadrī, wa jalā'a ḥuznī, wa dhahāba hammī.

O Allah, I am Your servant, son of Your servant, son of Your maidservant. My forelock is in Your hand. Your command over me is forever executed and Your decree over me is just. I ask You by every name that You have — which You have named Yourself, or taught to any of Your creation, or revealed in any of Your books, or kept hidden in the knowledge of the unseen with You — that You make the Quran the spring of my heart, the light of my chest, the remover of my sadness, and the repeller of my stress.

Source: Narrated by ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd (RA), reported by Aḥmad.

Background

The Prophet ﷺ said: "No one is ever afflicted with worry or grief and says this except that Allah will remove his worry and grief and replace it with joy." The companions asked: "Should we learn it?" He said: "Yes, everyone who hears it should learn it."

Breaking Down the Du'ā

PhraseWhat It Means
I am Your servant, son of Your servant You start with total humility — your entire lineage belongs to Allah. You, your father, your mother — all servants of His.
My forelock is in Your hand You have no power, no control, no ability except through Him. He is in complete control of you. When you internalise that, your heart finds rest — you stop fighting reality.
Your command over me is forever executed Whatever Allah has decreed will happen. What missed you could never have reached you and what reached you could never have missed you.
Your decree over me is just There is no injustice in it — even the sadness you are feeling right now. Whatever happened to cause it, Allah is just in His decree.
I ask You by every name that You have You invoke every name of Allah — those He revealed in His books, those He taught to any of His creation, and those He kept hidden in the unseen. Not one name, not ten — all of them.
Make the Quran the spring of my heart Spring brings life after a barren winter. You are asking Allah to make the Quran do that for your heart — bring it back to life. This is not just a du'a to remove anxiety; it is a du'a to connect you to the Quran so the Quran removes it. The medicine is the Quran — but you have to actually read it.

Takeaway

Your body is nourished by food and water, but your soul is nourished by Quran, prayer, and dhikr. Whoever turns away from Allah's remembrance will have a constricted life. Memorise this du'a, say it when worry or grief strikes — and pair it with a daily portion of the Quran.
